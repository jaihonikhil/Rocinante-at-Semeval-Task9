Table shows the individual ratings of two radiologists (R 1/R 2) for all four subjects (S 1, S 2, S 3, and S 4) and the first quartile 1 (Q 1), median, and third quartile (Q 3) calculated from the ratings of both radiologist. Overall CT and FRACTURE MRI yielded greatest Liker-scale ratings. FRACTURE MRI was equivalent to CT for detecting inwardly beveled fractures, outwardly beveled fractures and radiating fracture lines. Compared to FRACTURE MRI, T2- and T1-weighted MRI yielded lowest Likert-scale ratings. T1-weighted MRI was not performed in subject 1 and 2. Subject 2 did not present inwardly or outwardly beveled fractures at the temples. None of the individuals presented with keyhole fractures.
